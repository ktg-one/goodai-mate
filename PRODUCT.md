# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Primary users are owners and operators of small-to-medium businesses in Perth, Western Australia — typically tradies, service businesses, and local teams with $1M–$30M turnover. They are time-poor, practical, and drowning in repetitive admin, invoicing, scheduling, follow-ups, and systems that don't talk to each other.

They use the site when they are frustrated with "the boring stuff" eating their evenings and weekends. They want relief they can trust, delivered by people who understand real operations, not another SaaS pitch deck.

Secondary audiences include bookkeepers, office managers, and partners who influence buying decisions inside these businesses.

## Product Purpose

Good'ai exists to give small business owners their time back. We take the repetitive, soul-crushing admin and workflow friction off their plate so they can knock off early, spend time with family, and run the business instead of being run by it.

The site itself is the primary brand experience. The hero is the Voice Agent itself — a functional, local-first conversation where visitors can literally speak their admin problem and hear Good'ai respond in the brand voice (powered by local Supertonic during development). This is the product demonstration, not decoration. Everything below it supports the outcomes this agent + the automations behind it deliver.

Success looks like: qualified Perth businesses reaching out because the voice, the tone, and the demonstrated capability feel like a switched-on local mate who actually gets it.

## Brand Personality

**Switched-on Aussie mate.** Casual, warm, direct, practical, slightly cheeky, zero corporate polish.

- We speak like a reliable tradie who happens to be unusually good at systems.
- Short sentences. "We", never "I".
- We name problems plainly and offer relief without hype.
- We are competent but never slick. Expert but never arrogant.

**Three-word core personality:** Practical. Warm. Direct.

**Emotional goal for visitors:** Relief + quiet confidence. "These people will actually sort it so I can go home on time."

## Anti-references

This must never feel like:
- Generic SaaS landing pages (glassmorphism, gradient heroes, purple/teal palettes, "AI-powered" everything, stock robot imagery, vague "transform your workflow" copy).
- Corporate automation vendors (enterprise language, "leverage", "optimize", "synergistic", long forms, "submit your inquiry").
- Cold minimalist design or Anthropic-style editorial restraint (warm cream overload, excessive negative space, hushed typography).
- Tech-bro automation theatre (over-animated dashboards, fake AI demos that feel dishonest, heavy motion for its own sake).

We explicitly reject anything that makes small business owners feel talked down to or sold to.

## Design Principles

1. **The Voice Agent is the product.** The hero is a real, working voice conversation (local Supertonic ASR + Good'ai persona + spoken reply). Visitors should feel they just met the actual product, not a marketing asset. Every other section exists to explain what happens after the conversation.

2. **One orange per surface.** The brand shout is precious. Use it with intent for CTAs and key moments only. Ink and paper do the heavy lifting.

3. **Practical before pretty.** Every interaction, label, and piece of copy must be useful first. Beauty comes from clarity and honest materials (stamp shadows, hard edges, readable type), not decoration.

4. **We, not I.** The entire experience speaks in the collective voice of a small, capable Perth team that takes ownership of the boring stuff.

5. **1995 Yellow Pages directness, 2026 execution.** Bold, functional, slightly loud in the right places. Hard edges, visible structure, no blur, no gradients. Fast, readable, and unmistakably local.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.2 AA minimum as baseline (we aim higher on contrast and focus states because the brand uses strong ink-on-paper relationships).
- Full keyboard operability on every interactive element (voice controls, forms, nav).
- Reduced motion respected everywhere — the voice feature and any marquees must have static fallbacks.
- Touch targets minimum 44px.
- No information conveyed by colour alone.
- Voice feature must remain usable for people with motor or speech differences (text input path always available and first-class).
- Language is plain Australian English. No jargon that excludes non-technical users.
