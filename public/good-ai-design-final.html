Voice. Tone. Vibe.

How we sound

The pitch, in one breath

"AI for tradies, bakeries, brokers and bookkeepers — not Silicon Valley."

We speak like the bloke at the counter who actually knows the answer. No deep-tech jargon, no Bay Area buzzwords, no "synergies." We're optimistic without being naïve, blunt without being rude, and we always — always — tell you what something costs before we start.

04

Shape language. From the logo, into everything.

Circles · half-circles · triangles · bars

Rules of the road

  • The shape vocabulary comes straight out of the logo. Use it for bullets, dividers, status dots and decoration — not as a substitute for icons.
  • Pair one ink shape with one orange shape per composition. Two oranges in close range is a yelling match.
  • The split disc is the "AI mark." Use it where the brand says hello — favicons, app icons, badges.
  • Triangles point forwards. Bars terminate things. Half-circles bounce. Stop them doing anything else.

Off-limits

  • No gradients on the shapes. Flat fills only.
  • No 3D, no bevel, no drop-glow. (We did 1995 already.)
  • Never recreate the swan in pieces. The swan is the swan.
